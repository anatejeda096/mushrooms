Humanity’s relationship with mushrooms spans the entirety of our species’ existence. Neolithic Man used mushrooms for food, medicine, and as tinder for starting the fires. In Ancient Egypt, mushrooms were regarded as plants of immortality, which were given to people by the god Osiris. They appear in Egyptian hieroglyphics depicted as gifts from the gods and their use was forbidden to all but the pharaohs. This was also the rule in ancient China, where they were thought to provide inmortality and were served only to the Emperor.

Nordic shamans used Amanita muscaria to communicate with their gods, and Vikings consumed hallucinogenic mushrooms before battle, to work themselves into violent frenzies. Ergot, a fungus that infects grains, was a central component in early Greek fertility festivals and rites of spring.
